The USPS is like magic. 

For the price of the stamp, it will pick up a handwritten envelope from your home, decipher the address, and deliver it across country or across the street. The same thing with a private carrier would require a computerized label and cost $10+. 

From Esquire | The Complete Archive on 2020 04 13
> From the moment Benjamin Franklin was appointed the first postmaster general in 1775, the purpose of the post office has always been to bind the nation together. It was a way of unifying thirteen disparate colonies so that the abolitionist in Philadelphia had access to the same information and newspapers as the slaveholder in Augusta, Georgia.
